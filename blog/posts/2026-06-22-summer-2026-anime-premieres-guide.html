Summer 2026 Anime: Every Major Premiere and Release Date

The July season is stacked. Long-awaited sequels, a fresh batch of premieres, and 40-plus shows arriving in a single two-week window. Here is what is coming, when it drops, and how to keep it all straight.

Quick answer: The Summer 2026 anime season is led by new seasons of Re:ZERO, That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime, Mushoku Tensei: Jobless Reincarnation, Saga of Tanya the Evil, and The Elusive Samurai, alongside fresh premieres like Black Torch, Jaadugar: A Witch in Mongolia, and Kaiju Girl Caramelise. Most series begin the week of July 1 to 8. Re:ZERO Season 4 arrives later, on August 12.

Summer is one of the busiest stretches on the anime calendar, and 2026 is no exception. Dozens of series premiere within days of each other in early July, mixing the return of heavyweight franchises with brand-new shows from studios like Science SARU, CloverWorks, and Studio Bind. The good news is that the lineup is generous. The catch is that following it all is genuinely hard when everything launches at once.

Below is the rundown: the biggest returning series, the most promising new premieres, and a full premiere calendar with dates so you can plan your watchlist before July 1. Premiere dates reflect Crunchyroll's announced subtitled airtimes and may shift; we will keep this page current as schedules firm up.

When does the Summer 2026 anime season start?

The season front-loads hard. A few titles sneak out in late June, but the real wave lands across the first full week of July, roughly July 1 through July 8, when the majority of new and returning shows premiere. A second, smaller cluster trails into mid-July, and a couple of marquee titles hold back: Though I Am an Inept Villainess opens July 12, and Re:ZERO Season 4 waits until August 12. If you only block out one window to get organized, make it the last week of June.

The biggest returning series

Sequels are the spine of this season, and several of them are to some of the most popular isekai and fantasy franchises running.

Mushoku Tensei: Jobless Reincarnation Season 3 (Studio Bind, July 5) continues Rudeus into a new chapter of powerful foes and hard choices, and remains one of the genre's most acclaimed adaptations. That Time I Got Reincarnated as a Slime Season 4 (eightbit, Fridays in July) pushes Rimuru's nation-building saga forward. Saga of Tanya the Evil Season 2 (NUT, July 8) finally returns Tanya Degurechaff to the battlefield after a long wait. The Elusive Samurai Season 2 (CloverWorks, July) brings back one of the most visually striking historical series of recent years.

Rounding out the returners: Re:ZERO -Starting Life in Another World- Season 4 (WHITE FOX, August 12), Trapped in a Dating Sim Season 2 (ENGI, July 8), Skeleton Knight in Another World Season 2 (Aura Studio, July 4), and the ever-present One Piece (Toei Animation), which keeps sailing on Sundays. Long-running continuations like Daemons of the Shadow Realm also carry over from spring.

The most anticipated new premieres

If you prefer to start something fresh rather than catch up on four prior seasons, the new shows give you a clean entry point.

Black Torch (100studio, July 4) is the season's flagship new action title, following a reluctant ninja and animal whisperer named Jiro whose world collides with a spectral cat named Rago. Jaadugar: A Witch in Mongolia (Science SARU, July 4) is the most distinctive premiere on paper, a historical fantasy set in the 13th century following Sitara as she resists the Mongolian invasion of her homeland, from a studio known for bold visual style. Kaiju Girl Caramelise (LIDENFILMS, July 2) turns a high-school romance monstrous when its lead transforms into a kaiju whenever she falls in love, and Tomb Raider King (Studio EEK, July 8) delivers a second-chance power fantasy for action fans.

How to actually keep track of a 40-show season

Here is the part nobody warns you about. The lineup above is exciting in a list and overwhelming in practice. Forty-plus series premiere in roughly two weeks, each on its own weekday, some subbed and dubbed on different schedules, several of them sequels you may need a refresher on. Miss the first few days and you are suddenly behind on six shows at once.

The habit that works is simple: before the season starts, pick the handful of shows you actually care about, write down each one's premiere date and weekly slot, and track episodes as they air rather than trying to remember where you left off. A plain calendar does the job. A dedicated seasonal tracker does it better, because the friction of juggling release dates across multiple platforms is exactly what causes people to drop shows they meant to watch.

It is a small problem with an outsized effect on how much of a season you enjoy, and it is one we have been thinking hard about at IROIRO. For now, the move is to get your list down on paper this week. Premiere day comes fast.

The bottom line

Summer 2026 is a sequel-heavy season with enough new blood to keep things fresh. Mushoku Tensei, Slime, Tanya, and The Elusive Samurai anchor the returning slate; Black Torch and Jaadugar lead the newcomers; and Re:ZERO closes the season out in August. Almost everything lands the first week of July, so the single most useful thing you can do is sort your watchlist before July 1. Set your dates, pick your shows, and you will spend the season watching instead of scrambling to catch up.
